---
title: "Billing & plans"
description: "Manage your Dory subscription and plan limits."
---

Billing applies to the **cloud/web version** of Dory. If you are running Dory via Docker and have not configured Stripe environment variables, billing is disabled and all features are available with no limits.

<Note>
  Self-hosted Docker deployments are free with no plan restrictions. Billing only activates when the `STRIPE_SECRET_KEY` and related Stripe environment variables are set. If those variables are absent, the Billing page is hidden and no limits are enforced.
</Note>

## Plans

Dory has two plans:

| Plan | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Hobby** | The default plan for every new organization. Suitable for individuals and small teams getting started. |
| **Pro** | Paid plan with expanded limits. Managed through Stripe. |

When no active Stripe subscription is present, the organization is on Hobby. The organization returns to Hobby if a Pro subscription is canceled.

## Viewing your current plan

Navigate to **Settings > Billing** to see:

- **Current plan** — Hobby or Pro
- **Subscription status** — the status Stripe reports for your subscription (`active`, `trialing`, `past_due`, `canceled`, etc.)
- **Stripe subscription ID** — the identifier for your subscription in Stripe
- **Current period end** — the date your current billing period ends

<Info>
  The **Billing** tab only appears in **Settings** when billing is enabled for your deployment. On self-hosted Docker without Stripe configuration, this tab is not shown.
</Info>

## Upgrading to Pro

Only organization **owners** can upgrade, cancel, or manage billing.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open billing settings">
    Go to **Settings > Billing**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Upgrade to Pro">
    If your organization is on Hobby, the **Upgrade to Pro** button is visible. Click it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete checkout in Stripe">
    Dory redirects you to a Stripe-hosted checkout page. Enter your payment details and complete the purchase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to Dory">
    After a successful payment, Stripe redirects you back to **Settings > Billing**. The plan display updates to **Pro** once Stripe confirms the subscription is active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing your subscription

If your organization has an active Pro subscription, the **Manage billing** button appears in **Settings > Billing**. Clicking it opens the Stripe billing portal, where you can:

- Update your payment method
- View past invoices
- Cancel your subscription

<Warning>
  Only the organization **owner** can access the Manage billing button. Members with other roles see a read-only view of the current plan.
</Warning>

## Cancellation

When you cancel a Pro subscription through the Stripe portal, the cancellation is scheduled for the end of the current billing period. During this time:

- The plan remains **Pro** until the period ends
- A notice appears in **Settings > Billing** showing the scheduled cancellation date
- After the period ends, the organization reverts to **Hobby**

## Subscription status reference

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `active` | Subscription is current and paid. |
| `trialing` | In a trial period; Pro features are active. |
| `past_due` | Payment failed; subscription remains provisionally active while Stripe retries. |
| `incomplete` | Initial payment not yet confirmed. |
| `incomplete_expired` | Initial payment window expired; the subscription was never activated. |
| `unpaid` | All payment retries exhausted; organization is on Hobby. |
| `canceled` | Subscription has ended; organization is on Hobby. |
| `paused` | Subscription is paused. |

For statuses other than `active` or `trialing`, the organization is treated as Hobby until Stripe reports a valid active or trialing state.

## Self-hosted configuration

To **enable** billing on a self-hosted Docker deployment, set the required Stripe environment variables in your environment. To **disable** billing entirely, omit or unset those variables — the Billing settings tab will be hidden and no plan restrictions will apply.

```bash docker run example
docker run \
  -e STRIPE_SECRET_KEY=sk_live_... \
  -e STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET=whsec_... \
  # other env vars...
  getdory/dory:latest
```

Without `STRIPE_SECRET_KEY`, billing is disabled regardless of any other configuration.
